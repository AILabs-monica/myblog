<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">At 5:21pm Eastern on June 12, 2026, a letter arrived at Anthropic's offices. It came from the US government, citing national security and export control authorities, demanding immediate suspension of all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for any foreign national. The net effect: both models had to be disabled for everyone. Simon Willison had a script polling the API. At 6:59pm Pacific, claude-fable-5 abruptly returned a 404: "Claude Fable 5 is not available."</p>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">I've been following AI model deployments for years, and I've never seen anything like this. A commercially deployed model — arguably the most capable general-release model ever built — forcibly recalled by the government within days of launch.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-4 text-slate-900" style="font-size:15px;">The "Jailbreak" Was Asking The Model To Find Bugs</h2>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">The government's rationale was a method to "jailbreak" Fable 5. Anthropic investigated and found this essentially consisted of asking the model to read a specific codebase and fix any software flaws. Anthropic's statement was blunt: they validated that the same capability is available from OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and is "used every day by the defenders who keep systems safe." The government's standard, if applied consistently, would mean grounding every frontier model deployment.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-4 text-slate-900" style="font-size:15px;">The Irony: Anthropic Asked For This Power</h2>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">Two days before the shutdown, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei published "Policy on the AI Exponential," calling for FAA-style regulation of frontier AI. What he got was a letter at 5:21pm with no technical discussion, no hearing, no transparency. The government used the power Anthropic advocated for but discarded every safeguard Anthropic insisted should accompany it.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-4 text-slate-900" style="font-size:15px;">The Real Story: Who Benefits?</h2>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">While the US government was shutting down its own most capable AI, VentureBeat published stories about Kimi K2.7-Code — Moonshot AI's latest open-source coding model — and Xiaomi's MiMo Code, an open-source agent harness that beats Claude Code. DeepSeek V4 launched in May. GPT-5.5 topped the ALE benchmark at 24.0% while Fable 5 scored 22.0%. The AI race is more competitive than ever, and the US government just declared its own champion ineligible.</p>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">Three immediate effects: First, trust in closed-source US models takes a hit. If you're an enterprise, what happens if your chosen model gets recalled on 4 hours' notice? Second, open-source AI gets a geopolitical tailwind. Nobody in Washington can flip a switch and take DeepSeek V4's downloadable weights away from you. Third, multi-model architecture goes from best practice to survival requirement.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-4 text-slate-900" style="font-size:15px;">The Deeper Question</h2>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">If the standard is "it must be impossible to jailbreak," we will never deploy another frontier model. Anthropic's approach was defense in depth — strong safeguards, extensive red-teaming, aggressive monitoring. The government's action applied a binary standard: any jailbreak means the model gets pulled. The saddest part? Anthropic is probably the AI company that has invested most heavily in safety research — and they were the first to get shut down.</p>

<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold mt-10 mb-4 text-slate-900" style="font-size:15px;">What I Think Happens Next</h2>

<p class="mb-4" style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.8;">This incident marks a turning point. Before June 12, the AI vendor selection matrix had four columns: performance, cost, latency, ease of use. Now there's a fifth: geopolitical regulatory risk. The open-source AI ecosystem — particularly the rapidly maturing Chinese models — stands to gain the most. When you make your own best products unreliable through regulatory unpredictability, the market finds alternatives. It always does.</p>
